Alternative Travel Destinations in India

Instead of Leh and Ladakh: Spiti in Himachal Pradesh

Address: Spiti Valley, Marango Rangarik, Himachal Pradesh 172114

Get directions

When it comes to high altitude desert in India, you've most likely heard of Leh and Ladakh. But what about lesser-known Spiti? Bordered by Ladakh in the north, Tibet to the east, Kinnaur to the southeast, and the Kullu Valley to the south, Spiti has an average height of around 12,500 feet above sea level. This stark alpine desert land is scattered with small villages and monasteries, and enclosed by soaring peaks crowned with snow.

Instead of the Kerala Backwaters: Majuli in Assam

Address: Majuli, Assam

Get directions

Majuli, a large river island in Assam, offers a unique cultural experience with its traditional festivals and tribal communities. It's an ideal alternative to the Kerala Backwaters for those looking to explore lesser-known destinations in India.

Instead of Darjeeling: Kalimpong in West Bengal

Kalimpong is the perfect solution for escaping the crowds at Darjeeling. This peaceful town is perched on a remote ridge in the Himalayan foothills, about two-and-a-half hours away from Darjeeling, near the Sikkim border. Instead of Varanasi: Maheshwar in Madhya Pradesh

Address: Maheshwar, Madhya Pradesh 451224, India

Get directions

Often referred to as the "Varanasi of Central India," the small holy town of Maheshwar is set along the banks of the Narmada River and is also dedicated to Lord Shiva. Stroll along the ghats, watch local life, and take a sunset boat ride along the river and out to Baneshwar temple.

Instead of Hampi: Mandu in Madhya Pradesh

Mandu can be visited in conjunction with Maheshwar, as it's only a couple of hours away. This abandoned city from the Mughal era is commonly labeled the Hampi of Central India because of its treasure trove of ruins. They're spread over a 2,000 foot-high hilltop and enclosed by a 28-mile (45-kilometer) stretch of wall. In recognition of its importance, in 2018, the Indian government jointly named Mandu as Best Heritage City in India.

Instead of Fatehpur Sikri: Champaner-Pavagadh in Gujarat

Address: Champaner, Gujarat 389360, India

Get directions

These days, the abandoned 16th-century Mughal capital of Fatehpur Sikri near Agra is overrun by touts. A lesser-known, short-lived, capital that's also a UNESCO World Heritage site is Champaner-Pavagadh, situated about 90 miles southeast of Ahmedabad. It's the only unaltered and complete Islamic pre-Mughal city in India.

Instead of Jaisalmer: Osian in Rajasthan

Address: Osian, Rajasthan 342303, India

Get directions

Osian is a less touristy alternative to the golden city of Jaisalmer. This ancient desert town, about an hour from Jodhpur, is famous for its Hindu and Jain temples dating back to the 8th to 11th centuries. It also offers an authentic desert experience without the crowds.

Instead of Rishikesh: Vashisht in Himachal Pradesh

Address: Vashisht, Manali, Himachal Pradesh 175103, India

Get directions

Vashisht, a peaceful village near Manali in Himachal Pradesh, is an ideal alternative to Rishikesh for yoga and meditation enthusiasts. It offers hot springs, temples, and stunning views of the Himalayas.
